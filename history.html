General History of Wi-Fi

  • 1985: FCC Ruling on ISM Bands
    • U.S. Federal Communications Commission (FCC) releases parts of the ISM radio bands for unlicensed use for communications.
    • The frequency of the ISM radio bands were 2.4 Hz, same as microwave ovens, thus concluding that microwaves can interfer with ISM radio bands.
  • 1991: Invention of Precursor to 802.11 (Early Stages of Wi-Fi) in the Netherlands
    • A company known as NCR and AT&T made the early version of Wi-Fi, calling it WaveLAN, for cash registers.
    • Vic Hayes and Bruce Tuch approach IEEE (Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers) to create a standard, contribute to designing initial 802.11b and 802.11a specifications.
  • 1992: Prototype WLAN Test Bed in Australia
    • In Australia, a team led by John O'Sullivan at CSIRO develops a prototype test bed for a wireless local area network (WLAN) (Moving closer to modern Wi-Fi) in 1992.
  • 1997: First Wi-Fi Rules Released
    • Wi-Fi officially gets released to the public.
    • The first set of rules for Wi-Fi (called 802.11) released in 1997. Allowing devices to talk at a speed of up to 2 million bits per second (2 Mbps).
    • Wi-Fi speed upgraded in 1999 with 802.11b to permit 11 Mbit/s link speeds.
  • 1999: Wi-Fi Gets a Name and Apple Jumps In
    • Creation of the Wi-Fi Alliance to hold the Wi-Fi trademark for where most IEEE 802.11 products are sold.
    • Apple adopts Wi-Fi for iBook laptops in collaboration with key contributors to the Wi-Fi IEEE standard.
    • Wi-Fi becomes widely recognized with the branding "AirPort."
  • Wi-Fi Patents and Cont4roversies
    • Multiple organizations are responsible for the invention of Wi-Fi, including Australia, the United States, Netherlands.
    • However conroversies occured as the different countries claim the original invention of Wi-Fi to be theirs.
    • At the end, in 2009, Australia ended up being creditted with the idea and was awarded $200 million.
