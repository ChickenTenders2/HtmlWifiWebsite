Functionality of Wi-Fi

Introduction

Wi-Fi is a wireless networking technology that allows the internet to be accessed by devices such as computers, mobile devices, and other equipments
such as printers, and video cameras. According to Data Reportal, 65.7% of the world's total population (5.30 billion) as of 2023, have used or is using Wi-Fi.

Basic Functionality

Internet connectivity of devices occurs through a wireless router. Wireless routers are sometimes also referred to as a wireless local area network
(WLAN) device. Routers are hardwares that internet service providers use to connect your devices to their cable/internet network. When you access Wi-Fi,
you are connecting to a wireless router that allows your Wi-Fi-compatible devices to interface with the internet. Devices connected to the internet
are able to exchange information with one another.

Application

As we are all aware, Wi-Fi has made its mark in everyone's daily lives, people with Wi-Fi are able to complete digital actions which satisfied their
entertainment needs, or improved their productivity. Here are some of the applications of Wi-Fi in everyone's daily lives:

  • Mobile Applications
    • Transfer information and sharing files/documents on the go as long as Wi-Fi is connected.
    • Browsing the internet on mobile.
    • Telecommunication between different mobile devices.
    • Allowing video conferencing, thus connect with people from all around the world with ease.
    • Playing online mobile games.
  • Business Applications
    • Enabling workforce in various businesses to carry out daily operations more effectively and efficiently.
    • Expands businesses globally (promoting globalisation).
  • Home Applications
    • Operating with devices supporting Wi-Fi smoothly wirelessly without worrying on cables and wiring.
    • Allowing an exposure of the world's information right at the comfort of people's homes.
    • Allowing employees or students to work from home without losing efficiency.
  • Computer Applications
    • Provides wireless technological facilities such as ending emails, conducting research, designing graphics, curating content, and web browsing.
  • Industrial Applications
    • Efficiency support for industrial logistics: Reducing human intervention by communicating online via Wi-Fi to direct vehicles, cargos, materials transported, and scheduling of deliveries.

Advantages and Limitations

  • Advantage 1: Mobility and Flexibility - Within the coverage area of the WLAN, users can connect to the internet wirelessly.

  • Advantage 2: Cost-effective - Wireless services help save costs for businesses.

  • Advantage 3: Scalability - Easily expandable to allow more users and devices.

  • Advantage 4: Increased Productivity - Allowing work from home, increasing communication speed between employees.
  • Disadvantage 1: Security Concerns - Vulnerable to hacking, thus losing sensitive information.

  • Disadvantage 2: Limited Coverage Area - Users can only connect a Wi-Fi network within the coverage area.

  • Disadvantage 3: Interference - Microwaves and Cordless Phones can interfere with the Wi-Fi's radio frequence, reducing performance.

  • Disadvantage 4: Limited Bandwidth - Can supported limited number of devices at a time.
